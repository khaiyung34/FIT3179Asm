The Health Consequences of Smoking

Deaths caused by Smoking per 100,000 person

This map shows the number of deaths per 100,000 people caused by smoking. As shown on the map, the number deaths caused by smoking is relatively high around the whole world especially in Asia and Europe where the death rate is above average. This means that we should raise more awareness about the side effects of smoking in the Asian education.

How does GDP per Capita affect Percentage of Adult Smoker?

This chart shows the percentage of adult smoker against GDP per capita in 2019. In this scatterplot, we can see that smoking is very prominent in Europe . There might be a slight relationship between GDP per capita and percentage of adult smoker because Europe has almost the highest GDP per capita and their percentage of adult smoker is also the highest. The reason for this is mainly because cigarettes are heavily taxed, and therefore it is expensive to buy cigarettes unless the citizens have a lot of disposable income. This is also a proof that the tax disincentive works and the Europe governments should consider raising their tax on cigarettes to fight against smoking.

Who is affected the most by Smoking?

As shown in the graph, smoking affects older generation of smokers more because smoking causes long term effects that you might not feel immediately. This means that you will never know it is affecting you until it becomes fatal/serious. This is extremely dangerous because you might think it is not affecting you so much but in reality it is slowly burning out your lifespan.

Death rates caused by lung cancer in terms of Gender

As shown on the graph, lung cancer is generally more common amongst men because very high percentage of men smokes and be around smokers which is one of the main causes for lung cancer. An interesting observation can be made is that in Netherlands, the death rate of female caused by lung cancer has been increasing in a steady rate for the past two decades. A reason for this could be smoking for female has been normalised in Europe and hence causing more female to start smoking.
